Hi! I'm a third-year undergraduate student at the Georgia Institute of Technology, pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics and a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. I am fortunate to work with Prof. Yongxin Chen. My research interests are in theoretical machine learning, with a focus on generative modeling and mathematical foundations. Outside of academics, I enjoy photography, writing, reading, and bouldering. This past summer, I participated in a REU at Williams College.

Research

My research focuses on developing theoretical foundations for machine learning. My recent work includes exploring diffusion models on Riemannian manifolds for non-Euclidean generative modeling, generalizing Wasserstein gradient flows to arbitrary f-divergence functionals, and establishing nonasymptotic convergence bounds for sequential Monte Carlo samplers. I am also interested in differential geometry, natural language processing, and high-dimensional statistics.
