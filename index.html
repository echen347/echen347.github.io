Research

My primary research interests lie at the intersection of generative modeling, optimal transport, and differential geometry. I focus on the algorithmic and theoretical foundations of diffusion models, particularly on how to build them on general Riemannian manifolds such as Lie groups. This work often involves leveraging strong structural priors from optimal transport to create more stable and interpretable models. I am also interested in accelerating sampling for discrete diffusion models for structured data, with applications to protein modeling. My other interests include high-dimensional statistics, NLP research, applied machine learning, and AI interpretability.
