Hi! I'm a rising third-year mathematics undergraduate at the Georgia Institute of Technology, where I am fortunate to work with Prof. Amirali Aghazadeh. Briefly, my research interests are in theoretical machine learning, and mildly in mathematics and theoretical computer science. Outside of academics, I enjoy photography, writing, reading, and bouldering. This summer, I am participating in a REU at Williams College.

Research

My research interests center on developing and understanding machine learning models that are grounded in mathematical first principles. In generative modeling, my interests include the mathematical theory of diffusion models on general manifolds, the design of generative frameworks using optimal transport maps, and the algorithmic challenge of accelerating sampling for discrete processes, particularly for structured biological data like proteins. My work also extends to a fundamental interest in quantifying complexity. This involves computationally probing novel physical frameworks, such as Assembly Theory, to understand how they relate to traditional statistical measures and their unique sensitivity to an object's hierarchical and procedural structure.
