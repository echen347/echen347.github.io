Hi! I'm a third-year undergraduate student at the Georgia Institute of Technology, pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics and a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. I am fortunate to work with Prof. Yongxin Chen and Prof. Amirali Aghazadeh. My research deals with the theoretical foundations of machine learning, with a focus on application of advanced mathematics to generative models. Outside of academics, I enjoy photography, writing, reading, and bouldering. This past summer, I participated in a REU at Williams College.

Research

My research focuses on developing and understanding machine learning models from mathematical first principles. In generative modeling, I have explored the theoretical foundations for diffusion models on Riemannian manifolds, designed convexity-preserving normalizing flows using optimal transport, and worked on accelerating sampling for discrete processes, particularly for structured biological data. My work also involves generalizing Wasserstein gradient flows to arbitrary f-divergence functionals for molecular dynamics, establishing nonasymptotic convergence bounds for sequential Monte Carlo samplers on non-log-concave distributions, and developing statistical methods for estimating martingale optimal transport plans.

I am also interested in differential geometry, natural language processing, and high-dimensional statistics.
